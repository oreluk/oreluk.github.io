Instrument Models

Worked on validation and uncertainty quantification of coal combustion models and instrument models. When validating a computational model to experimental data, often the comparison is not one to one. Many confounding parameters are not present in the computational simulation. These parameters are hidden within the experimental data due to the derived nature of the experimental measurements. Experiments do not capture properties directly, they are processed (which may contain its own uncertainties). Turning a measured voltage or intensity signal into a temperature or species concentration is done through an instrument model, which may have many possible forms. In validation and uncertainty quantification, is necessary to model the experiment as well as the instrumentation process and explore all the possible model forms each may take. Only then we can accurately account for uncertainty, propagate the uncertainty forward, and make predictions.

Partially Stirred Reactor

Created a parallel version of PaSR: A Partially Stirred Reactor Model (legacy FORTRAN code). Our implementation of MPI in PaSR gave a 10x speed up on 24 threads and had nearly 65% strong-scaling efficiency at 48 threads. This project enabled for complex reactions mechanisms, which were previously computationally intractable, to be investigated in future research. Codes are not available online due to software licensing.


StackOverflow Tag Classification

Built a predictive model to determine which tags belong to particular posts on Stackoverflow. Wrote a python script to take HTML from Stackoverflow and generated a word term matrix for training. Derived unique features to gain the more information from the training data. Extensive testing of various models and ensembles were conducted to determine the best performing model through cross validation. A tuned xgboost model provided good performance and a was first place for the multi-class classification project.
